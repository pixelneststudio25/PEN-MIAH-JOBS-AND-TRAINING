People Skills

What Are People Skills?

People skills are the tools we use to interact effectively with others. They encompass communication, empathy, conflict resolution, and collaboration. In the workplace, strong people skills lead to better teamwork, higher customer satisfaction, and smoother operations. Outside work, they enrich personal relationships and help build a strong network.

According to Business Dictionary, people skills are “a set of skills enabling a person to get along with others, to communicate ideas effectively, to resolve conflicts, and to achieve personal or business goals.” They are essential for every employee and leader.

Forbes’ Essential People Skills

Forbes highlights eight core people skills. Each can be developed with intention:

  • 1. The ability to relate to others

    Finding common ground and building rapport. Practice by asking open-ended questions and looking for shared interests.

  • 2. Strong communication skills

    Clearly expressing ideas verbally and in writing, and tailoring your message to your audience. Good communicators also pay attention to tone and body language.

  • 3. Patience with others

    Allowing people time to express themselves and learn at their own pace. Patience reduces friction and builds trust.

  • 4. Knowing how and when to show empathy

    Recognising others’ emotions and responding with care. Empathy strengthens connections and defuses tension.

  • 5. Active listening skills

    Fully concentrating, understanding, and remembering what the other person says. Active listeners make others feel valued.

  • 6. Genuine interest in others

    Asking about their lives, aspirations, and challenges – not just waiting for your turn to speak.

  • 7. Flexibility

    Adapting your approach to different personalities and situations. Rigidity often causes conflict; flexibility creates harmony.

  • 8. Good judgment

    Knowing what to say, when to say it, and when to remain silent. It involves reading the room and acting with tact.

Queen Esther's Three Rules for Dealing with People
  1. Understand that people are different. Everyone has unique backgrounds, values, and communication styles. Instead of expecting others to think like you, seek to understand their perspective. This reduces misunderstandings and builds respect.
  2. Choose your battles. Not every disagreement needs to become a conflict. Ask yourself: “Will this matter in a week? A month?” Let go of minor irritations to preserve energy for what truly counts.
  3. Always have the goal in mind when dealing with people. Whether you’re negotiating, collaborating, or simply chatting, keep your objective clear. This helps you stay focused and respond constructively rather than react emotionally.

“The single biggest problem in communication is the illusion that it has taken place.” – George Bernard Shaw

How to Sharpen Your People Skills
  • Practice active listening: In conversations, focus entirely on the speaker. Summarise what you heard to confirm understanding.
  • Observe non‑verbal cues: Body language, tone, and facial expressions often convey more than words.
  • Ask open‑ended questions: Encourage others to share by asking “how” and “why” instead of yes/no questions.
  • Seek feedback: Ask trusted colleagues, “How could I have handled that interaction better?”
  • Read books on emotional intelligence and communication: Knowledge gives you new techniques to try.
  • Step out of your comfort zone: Talk to people you don’t know well – it builds adaptability.
  • Reflect after difficult interactions: Identify what went well and what you’d do differently next time.
