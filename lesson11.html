Ethics

What Are Ethics?

Ethics is the discipline dealing with what is good and bad, and with moral duty and obligation. In a professional context, ethics refers to a code of conduct that guides behaviour – the principles that help us decide right from wrong. Organisations with strong ethical cultures build trust with clients, employees, and the public.

Key Ethical Principles
  • Confidentiality: Protecting sensitive information, not sharing it without consent.
  • Punctuality: Respecting others' time by being on time for meetings and deadlines.
  • Objectivity: Making decisions based on facts, not personal bias or favouritism.
  • Fairness: Treating all people equitably and without discrimination.
  • Integrity: Acting consistently with moral principles, even when no one is watching.
  • Honesty: Being truthful in communications and representations.
  • Reliability: Following through on commitments and being dependable.
Why Ethics Matter in the Workplace
  • Reputation: Ethical behaviour builds a positive brand image.
  • Employee morale: People want to work where they feel respected and proud.
  • Legal compliance: Many ethical standards are also legal requirements.
  • Customer loyalty: Clients prefer to do business with trustworthy organisations.
  • Long‑term success: Unethical shortcuts may yield short‑term gains but lead to long‑term failure.

“In looking for people to hire, you look for three qualities: integrity, intelligence, and energy. And if they don't have the first, the other two will kill you.” – Warren Buffett
