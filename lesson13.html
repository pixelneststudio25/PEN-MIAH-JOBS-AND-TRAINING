Planning and Decision Making

What Is Planning?

A plan is a detailed proposal for doing or achieving something – an intention or decision about what one is going to do. Planning is the process of thinking through the entire journey, anticipating obstacles, and mapping out the steps needed to succeed. As the saying goes: “What gets planned gets done. What gets written gets done. What gets talked about gets done. What gets trained for gets done.”

Planning and decision making work hand in hand. Effective planning involves answering questions like “what if?” and drawing clear mental pictures. The more detailed your plan, the greater your chance of success.

Execution: Bringing Plans to Life

Execution is the carrying out of a plan, order, or course of action. A brilliant plan is worthless without proper execution. The time invested in planning pays off when execution becomes smoother and more focused.

5 Ways to Better Execute Your Plan
  • Sharpen your Focus – Focus provides the clarity needed to make decisions that support your most important goals. It creates a clearly defined pathway to success. However, plan realistically; an unrealistic plan will frustrate execution.
  • Build your Competence – Competence here means having the right skills, knowledge, and resources. Assign tasks to people who are empowered and equipped to execute. Invest in training where gaps exist.
  • Ignite your Passion – Passion creates a sense of connectedness and purpose. When people care deeply about the outcome, they go the extra mile.
  • Do one thing at a time – Multitasking dilutes attention. Focus on completing one task before moving to the next. This increases quality and reduces errors.
  • Give careful instructions – Ambiguity leads to mistakes. Provide clear, concise directions and check for understanding.
The Decision-Making Process

Effective decision making is integral to planning. Use these steps to make better decisions:

  1. Identify the decision – Clearly define what you need to decide.
  2. Gather relevant information – Collect data, opinions, and feedback.
  3. Identify alternatives – Brainstorm multiple options.
  4. Weigh the evidence – Evaluate pros and cons of each alternative.
  5. Choose among alternatives – Select the option that best aligns with your goals.
  6. Take action – Implement your decision.
  7. Review your decision – Assess the outcome and learn for the future.
