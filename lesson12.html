Leadership Skills

What Is Leadership?

Leaders help themselves and others to do the right things. They set direction, build an inspiring vision, and create something new. Leadership is about mapping out where you need to go to "win" as a team or an organization; it is dynamic, exciting, and inspiring. Yet, while leaders set the direction, they must also use management skills to guide their people to the right destination in a smooth and efficient way.

Key Leadership Skills
  • Honesty and Integrity – Leaders must be trustworthy. People follow those who are truthful and act consistently with their values.
  • Ability to Inspire Others – A great leader paints a compelling picture of the future and motivates people to contribute.
  • Commitment and Passion – Enthusiasm is contagious. Leaders who are passionate about their work energise the team.
  • Good Communicator – Clear, empathetic communication ensures everyone understands goals and feels heard.
  • Decision-Making Capabilities – Leaders gather input, analyse options, and make timely decisions even under uncertainty.
  • Accountability – They take responsibility for outcomes – both successes and failures.
  • Delegation and Empowerment – Trusting others with tasks and authority builds capability and ownership.
  • Creativity and Innovation – Leaders encourage new ideas and are open to change.
Supervisory Skills
  • Communication: Clearly explain goals, tasks, and expectations. Good supervisors listen as much as they speak.
  • Motivation: Understand what drives each team member and create an environment where they want to excel.
  • Delegating: Assign tasks based on strengths and development needs, providing support without micromanaging.
  • Positivity: Maintain an optimistic outlook, especially during challenges; it sets the emotional tone.
  • Trustworthiness: Keep confidences, follow through on commitments, and treat everyone fairly.
  • Creativity: Find innovative solutions to problems and encourage others to share ideas.
  • Feedback: Give regular, constructive feedback – both praise and suggestions for improvement.
  • Responsibility: Own your decisions and their consequences; model accountability.
