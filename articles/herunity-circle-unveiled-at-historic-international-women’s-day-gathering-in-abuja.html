HERUNITY CIRCLE Unveiled at Historic International Women’s Day Gathering in Abuja

Thu Mar 12 2026 00:00:00 GMT+0000 (Coordinated Universal Time) PEN-MIAH Career Advice

The Unity Schools Old Students Association (USOSA) marked a historic milestone as it celebrated International Women’s Day with its first-ever physical gathering dedicated to female members. The prestigious event took place at the Shehu Musa Yar’Adua Conference Centre in Abuja, bringing together distinguished alumni, professionals, and leaders from across various sectors.

The occasion was particularly significant as it officially unveiled HERUNITY CIRCLE, the female chapter of USOSA. The initiative represents a new platform designed to strengthen collaboration, mentorship, and leadership among women who are alumni of Nigeria’s Unity Schools. The gathering symbolized progress, unity, and a renewed commitment to empowering women within the USOSA community.

Several historic “firsts” defined the event. Among them was the recognition of Barrister Chinedu Amure of Federal Government Girls’ College, Bakori, who serves as the first female Vice President of USOSA. The inauguration of HERUNITY CIRCLE itself also marked the creation of the first official female body within the association. In addition, the event represented the first physical meeting of female USOSANS, making it a landmark moment for the organization.

The keynote address was delivered by Professor Francesca Bassey, the first female Vice Chancellor of the University of Cross River State (UNICROSS). Her address highlighted the importance of women supporting one another, building strong professional networks, and continuing to break barriers across industries and leadership spaces.

The event also featured an impressive lineup of highly accomplished panelists drawn from academia, business, engineering, law, finance, media, and security. Among them was Professor Appolonia A. Okhimamhe of WASCAL Graduate Studies at the Federal University of Technology, Minna, who holds the distinction of being the first Nigerian woman to earn a PhD in Applied Remote Sensing. Also present was Professor Rabia Salihu Sa’id, an award-winning academic recognized with the Elsevier Foundation Award.

Representing the corporate and financial sector was Nnenna Anyim Okoro, Executive Director for Corporate Banking and South at Keystone Bank Limited. The panel also included Engr. Elozino Olaniyan, Managing Director and CEO of Midwestern Oil and Gas Limited, as well as Mrs. Wendy Nwenenda Kuku, SAN, Deputy Director in the Litigation and Prosecution Department of the Independent National Electoral Commission (INEC).

Other notable panelists included Leah Katung Babatunde, founder of Saban Media Services and Market Naija TV; Princess Ibilola Oluwa Awosika, Team Lead for HR Technology Strategy and Platform Ownership at NEOM; and Stoney Rollins Akpafi, an operations excellence professional recognized among the Top 100 Career Women in Africa.

The panel further featured Tennessee Haruna, a Security Risk Management Consultant who serves as liaison for Women in Security at ASOS International in Abuja; Francesca Naymarie Musa Akande, a seasoned legal practitioner and public servant currently serving as Managing Director and CEO of Kaduna Industrial and Finance Company Limited (KIFC); and Chioma Aninwe, a communications strategist and Founder and Chief Strategy Officer of INGAGE PR.

Also present at the event was the dynamic USOSA President, Mr. Mike Magaji, who currently serves as a Director at the Bureau of Public Enterprises. His presence underscored the association’s commitment to supporting and strengthening the role of women within the organization. Several male USOSANS also attended the event, demonstrating solidarity and encouragement for the pioneering efforts of their female counterparts.

The successful unveiling of HERUNITY CIRCLE marks the beginning of a new chapter for women within the Unity Schools Old Students Association. With such a strong foundation of accomplished leaders and professionals, the initiative is expected to foster mentorship, leadership development, and meaningful collaboration among female alumni.

As the celebration of International Women’s Day concluded, the atmosphere was filled with optimism and pride. The gathering not only honored the achievements of remarkable women but also set the stage for even greater milestones ahead. The trailblazing USOSANS behind HERUNITY CIRCLE have opened the door for future generations of women to lead, inspire, and achieve even greater heights.
