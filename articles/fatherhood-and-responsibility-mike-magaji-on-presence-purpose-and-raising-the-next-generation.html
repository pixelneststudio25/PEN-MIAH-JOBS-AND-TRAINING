Fatherhood and Responsibility: Mike Magaji on Presence, Purpose, and Raising the Next Generation

Tue Mar 17 2026 00:00:00 GMT+0000 (Coordinated Universal Time) Mike Magaji Career Advice

In a candid and thought-provoking conversation, Mike Magaji, President General of the Unity Schools Old Students Association (USOSA), shared deep insights on fatherhood, responsibility, and the long-term impact of parental presence on a child’s development.

Addressing the issue of absentee fathers, he described absence as a gap that inevitably leaves a mark. According to him, no matter the circumstances, there is no true substitute for a parent’s presence in a child’s life. While some children may grow up under the care of relatives such as grandparents or uncles, he emphasized that these figures, though supportive, cannot fully replace the role of a parent. There is a unique emotional connection a child experiences when both father and mother are actively involved, and its absence can create lasting psychological effects.

He explained that children who grow up without one parent often develop unanswered questions and internal struggles, regardless of how materially comfortable their lives may seem. The absence can lead to a sense of incompleteness, especially when they compare their experiences with those of their peers. Over time, this gap may manifest in emotional or behavioral challenges, reinforcing the importance of parental presence.

While acknowledging that life circumstances are not always ideal, he encouraged fathers to make intentional efforts to be present in their children’s lives. He challenged the notion that parenting is merely about financial provision, stressing that being physically and emotionally available is just as important. In his words, raising a child is not a short-term task but a long-term commitment, describing each child as a “21-year project” at the very least. This perspective highlights the level of dedication, time, and responsibility required to raise a well-rounded individual.

On the role of fathers who provide financially but lack personal connection with their children, he noted that while provision is important, it is not sufficient on its own. Parenting requires a balance. Mothers may naturally take on nurturing roles, but fathers also play a crucial part in shaping a child’s psychological and moral development. Guidance, conversations, and shared moments often leave lasting impressions that children carry into adulthood.

Reflecting on his own upbringing, he shared that while he was emotionally closer to his mother, his father played a significant role in shaping his mindset, particularly by encouraging him to read and learn widely. These kinds of influences, he noted, are what children remember and carry forward throughout their lives.

He further emphasized the importance of maximizing the time parents have with their children. Childhood is a limited window, and once it passes, it cannot be recovered. The values, discipline, and guidance instilled during those formative years often determine the direction a child takes later in life. In many societies, children become independent at a relatively young age, making early parental involvement even more critical.

Touching on societal issues, he pointed out that a lack of parental presence can sometimes contribute to negative outcomes. He noted that many individuals who end up on destructive paths often come from backgrounds where there was a deficiency in guidance, structure, or affection. While this is not always the case, it highlights the broader impact of parenting on society as a whole.

Speaking directly to younger fathers, he stressed the importance of responsibility. Parenting, he explained, is a serious commitment that requires careful thought and readiness. Beyond financial capability, factors such as time, emotional availability, age, and long-term planning all play a role. Raising a child involves years of consistent effort, from early childhood through education and into adulthood.

He also highlighted the realities of delayed parenting, noting that starting a family later in life comes with extended responsibilities that can stretch well into later years. This reinforces the need for intentional decision-making and preparedness when it comes to family life.

Ultimately, his message was clear: fatherhood is not just about creating life, but about nurturing and guiding it. Absence, whether physical or emotional, creates gaps that can affect not just the child, but society at large. Presence, on the other hand, builds confidence, stability, and a sense of belonging.

His closing message serves as a reminder that parenting is one of the most significant responsibilities an individual can undertake. The time, effort, and care invested in raising a child shape not only their future but also the kind of society we collectively build.
