Problem Solving Skills

What Is Problem Solving?

Problem solving is the process of working through details of a problem to reach a solution. It involves identifying the issue, analysing possible causes, generating options, and implementing the best solution. Strong problem‑solving skills are essential in every role – they demonstrate critical thinking, resourcefulness, and the ability to handle challenges independently.

The ISE Method: A Simple Framework

A practical three‑step approach to tackle any problem:

  • Identify – Clearly define the problem. Ask: What is happening? Where? When? Who is involved? A problem well stated is half solved. Also identify possible options – brainstorming without judgment.
  • Scrutinize – Examine each potential solution. Consider pros, cons, resources needed, and likely outcomes. Look for hidden assumptions or root causes.
  • Employ – Implement the best solution. Monitor results and be ready to adapt. If the chosen solution doesn't work, cycle back: re‑identify, re‑scrutinize, and try another approach.
Other Useful Techniques
  • Root Cause Analysis (5 Whys): Keep asking "why" until you uncover the fundamental cause.
  • Brainstorming: Generate many ideas without criticism, then evaluate.
  • Mind Mapping: Visually organise factors and relationships.
  • SWOT Analysis: Assess Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, and Threats related to the problem.
  • Reverse Thinking: Imagine you want to cause the problem – what would you do? Then reverse those actions.

“We cannot solve our problems with the same thinking we used when we created them.” – Albert Einstein

A Step‑by‑Step Problem‑Solving Process
  1. Define the problem clearly. Write it down in one sentence.
  2. Gather information. What do you already know? What do you need to find out?
  3. Identify possible causes. Use tools like the 5 Whys or fishbone diagram.
  4. Generate multiple solutions. Aim for at least three distinct options.
  5. Evaluate solutions. Consider feasibility, impact, and risks.
  6. Choose the best option. Decide based on evidence and priorities.
  7. Implement the solution. Create an action plan with timelines.
  8. Review and learn. After implementation, assess what worked and what could be improved next time.
